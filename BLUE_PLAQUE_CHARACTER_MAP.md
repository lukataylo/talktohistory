# Blue Plaque Character Map

Source-backed character candidates for TalkToHistory. The goal is not to ingest every London plaque on day one; English Heritage alone has 1,000+ London plaques. The product goal is a balanced, popular, walkable character layer that makes every part of London feel represented.

Primary sources used: English Heritage blue plaques, City of London plaque list, official venue/council plaque pages, and Plaques of London where the English Heritage scheme does not cover a site.

## Product Notes

- Make characters feel like people, not encyclopedia entries. Every pin needs a voice, a decision, and a tiny real-world action.
- Mix household names with discoveries. A route of only famous men feels stale; the app gets its edge from putting Bob Marley beside Ellen Craft, Harry Beck beside the Match Girls, and Ada Lovelace beside Mary Seacole.
- Use "district packs" for virality: Soho After Dark, Brixton Resistance, East End Radicals, Science in the Suburbs, Spies and Signals, Artists in Exile.
- Do not overpromise exact historical dialogue. Use "inspired by verified plaque/history sources" and keep stories clearly interpretive.
